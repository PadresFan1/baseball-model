1. THE DIAGNOSTIC BREAKTHROUGH SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Baseline Setup: Using 4 team-level rolling metrics (d_woba, d_xwoba_bat, 
  d_xfip, d_xwoba_pit) resulted in a persistent ~40% win rate across all 
  threshold sweeps.
- The Threshold Reality: Tightening filters (edge > 12%) spiked ROI to +6.1% 
  WITHOUT increasing win rate. This proved the model was serving as an 
  "underdog sniper"—uncovering long-shot payout value rather than becoming 
  more predictive.
- The Anchor Test (Market-Residual Approach): Feeding the de-vigged market 
  line directly into the model as a baseline feature caused win rate to leap 
  to ~49.7% across all thresholds, but overall profit went negative due to 
  volume starvation.
- The Ultimate Conclusion: Team-level rolling metrics are a pure noise model. 
  The closing market line has already completely digested them. To beat the 
  closing line, the features must change from top-down (team) to bottom-up 
  (player-specific matchups).

2. CORE METRIC RE-MAPPING (PLAYER-LEVEL PROJECTIONS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
You do not need new metrics; the 4 sabermetrics used are perfect, but they 
must be calculated at the individual player level using situational splits:

- Batter Metrics (d_woba, d_xwoba_bat): Must be tracked by Platoon Splits 
  (vs. LHP and vs. RHP). Compute each starting batter's rolling 100 Plate 
  Appearances (PA) specifically against the handedness of tonight's starting pitcher.
- Pitcher Metrics (d_xfip, d_xwoba_pit): Track tonight's specific starting 
  pitcher's individual rolling 50-70 innings pitched. 
- Bullpen Expected Delta: Isolate a rolling 14-day weighted bullpen xFIP for 
  the relief corps to handle the later innings.

3. THE MATHEMATICAL MATCHUP GRID (LOG5 / ODDS RATIO)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
For every game, simulate the 38 to 40 individual projected plate appearances 
by pairing each of the 9 starting batters against the starting pitcher. 

Combine individual Batter skill (B) and Pitcher skill (P) relative to the 
League Baseline (L) to calculate the projected plate appearance outcome (Pr):

  Pr = (B * P / L) / [ (B * P / L) + ((1 - B) * (1 - P) / (1 - L)) ]

Summing these individual probabilities creates a highly granular, un-diluted 
projected team run total that accounts for real-time lineup changes.

4. MODELING "OPENER" AND BULLPEN GAMES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Omitting opener games leaves a structural leak because sportsbooks price them 
accurately while team-level models read them blindly. 

Implementation Strategy:
1. Detect Openers: Flag games where the listed starting pitcher averages 
   fewer than 2.0 Innings Per Appearance (IPA) over his last 15 outings.
2. Inning Packages: Shift the model from a monolithic "Starting Pitcher" 
   block to an exposure-weighted composite pitching staff calculation:

   Projected xFIP = (1/9 * Opener xFIP) 
                    + (4.5/9 * Bulk Reliever xFIP) 
                    + (3.5/9 * Core Bullpen xFIP)
